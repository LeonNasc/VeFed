"Federated learning (FL) offers a promising approach for collaborative healthcare AI development without cross-institutional data sharing. However, evaluating FL under realistic epidemiological dynamics, data heterogeneity, and rare disease conditions remains challenging.
We propose a virtual epidemiological environment in which autonomous agents simulate the spread of infectious diseases across distributed healthcare silos. Symptomatic agents consult virtual physicians powered by large language models (LLMs), which generate diagnostic outputs based on patient-reported information. Each silo trains its local clinical model on institution-specific case distributions, reflecting non-IID and small-sample settings. In particular, this study will explore the diffusion of knowledge about emerging diseases under FL training and its impact on early detection of diseases on unseen silos.
This framework aims to enable the investigation of (i) inter-silo federated training of generative clinical agents, (ii) performance under rare and emerging disease scenarios, and (iii) cross-institutional knowledge diffusion for early detection of novel conditions. We evaluate diagnostic accuracy, robustness to imbalance, and adaptation to emergent disease patterns while analysing system behaviour under heterogeneous data regimes.
The proposed simulation provides a reproducible testbed for studying methodological and systemic aspects of federated learning in healthcare AI, supporting the development of secure, adaptive, distributed clinical intelligence systems."
